It is just in face of the vast difference that the need to define one's own limits and identity become a necessary deed.

In the field of aesthetics, Nicolás Bourriaud, describes something similar when he proposes the Altermodernity: A way of doing art that is “connected to the creolisation of cultures and the fight for autonomy, but also the possibility of producing singularities in a more and more standardized world.”. Bourriaud's perspective of art thrives for the separation and identification away from the hegemonic definition of all forms of art in order to be able to keep some sort of authentic voice in a world everyday more globlalized and where, all production of art is in danger of falling into the "hegemonic western discourse".

 Your cultural identity doesn’t become a thing until you leave your niche and get in contact with other bigger culture (The hegemonic depends on where you are?).
